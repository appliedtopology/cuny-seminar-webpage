title: "Quaternion Neural Networks Framework: Overview, Applications, and Future Development"
author: Sos Agaian
date: 2026-03-16
category: talk
---
The rapid expansion of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) has brought countless new architectures and has reshaped everyday technologies, from image and speech recognition to medical imaging and autonomous systems. Yet as these models grow more complex, real-valued networks often face challenges in computational cost, parameter efficiency, and handling naturally multidimensional data.
This talk offers an accessible overview of key areas in modern neural network research and highlights ongoing limitations. The main focus is on quaternion convolutional neural networks (QCNNs), which have gained momentum for outperforming real-valued CNNs in many computer vision and image‑processing tasks. QCNNs provide several key advantages: (i) efficient processing of multidimensional inputs and grouping related features (such as RGB channels) into a single quaternion unit, (ii) better modeling of internal dependencies across spatial, color, and structural features, (iii) reduced learning complexity, often using up to four times fewer parameters while lowering the risk of overfitting,  and  (iv) Improved structural representation, treating edges and shapes as cohesive geometric patterns rather than isolated features. Examples include -Encoding local feature relations, such as RGB interactions, which real-valued CNNs treat independently. Capturing structural patterns, edges, textures, and contours in a unified form. Improving generalization through more compact and expressive representations. The talk will also introduce a new QCNN framework developed for intelligent transportation systems, showing clear performance gains over state-of-the-art real-valued CNNs in tasks requiring reliable, real-time analysis.
